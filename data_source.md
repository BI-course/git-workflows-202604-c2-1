# Data Sources 

## 1. Internal Data Sources
These are data points generated within the organization's own systems.

 **Transactional Systems (ERP):** Enterprise Resource Planning systems that track daily operations like inventory levels, supply chain movements, and manufacturing stats.
 **Customer Relationship Management (CRM):** It's Data regarding customer interactions, sales history, and support tickets.
 **Financial Records:** It entails payroll, revenue reports, and expense logs found in accounting software.
 **Operational Logs:** These are web server logs or application usage data that track system performance.

## 2. External Data Sources
Data gathered from outside the organization to provide market context.

 **Market Research APIs:** These are third-party data providers that give insights into competitor pricing or consumer trends.
 **Social Media & Sentiment Data:** Public feeds from platforms like  Twitter, Instagram or Tiktok to gauge brand perception.
 **Open Government Data:** Demographic or economic statistics from national bureaus (e.g.KNBS for Kenya) used for geographic expansion planning.
 
## 3. Data Formats
To ensure the data is ready, we categorize it by structure:

**Structured** It is highly organized in rows and columns eg SQL Databases and Excel sheets. 
**Semi-Structured** Has some organizational properties but not a rigid schema eg  JSON files, XML. 
 **Unstructured**  Has no predefined format making it difficult to collect and analyze eg PDF reports, Video.
